Water Quality Parameters

There are several types of parameters which affect the water quality.
But as of now we are only taking these 3 parameters into consideration.

Chlorophyll

Chlorophyll a concentrations are an indicator of phytoplankton abundance and biomass in coastal and estuarine waters. They can be an effective measure of trophic status1, are potential indicators of maximum photosynthetic rate (P-max)2 and are a commonly used measure of water quality.

Total Phosphorous

Too much phosphorus can stimulate excess growth of algae, which leads to low dissolved oxygen levels, potential for harmful algal toxins, blockage of sunlight needed by organisms and plants in the water and degraded habitat conditions for benthic macroinvertebrates and other aquatic life

Secchi Disk Transparency

Secchi Disk Transparency is a quick, simple and accurate method for determining lake water quality. Transparency is a measurement of how deep into a lake the sunlight can penetrate..
